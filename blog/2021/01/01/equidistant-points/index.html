Equidistant points from a pair of points

January 01, 2021

In the figure below, visually, it is not too hard to believe that the points equidistant from uuu and vvv are exactly the the points on the dashed line. A description of the dashed line seems to be "the line running perpendicularly through the midpoint of the segment between uuu and vvv." This turns out to be true, and perhaps is a standard exercise in Euclidean geometry. However, we can formulate this using linear algebra and obtain a result that generalizes to higher dimensions, yet the proof is still "strategized" by our visual intuition.

Theorem. Let uuu and vvv be two points in Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn. m+span(v−u)⊥=Am + \text{span}(v-u)^\bot = Am+span(v−u)⊥=A
